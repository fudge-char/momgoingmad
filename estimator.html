Independent Construction Agreement

Terms of Agreement

Independent Relationship

The Contractor is an independent business, not an employee. The Contractor is responsible for its own taxes, obligations, insurance, employees, and operations.

Scope of Services

The Contractor may be engaged to perform: Construction supervision, Renovations, Capital improvements, Project management, Procurement, Trade coordination, Quality inspections, Scheduling, and Site safety management. Each project requires separate approval. No minimum work is guaranteed.

Contractor Responsibilities

The Contractor shall: Supply qualified personnel; Hire and supervise subcontractors; Maintain all necessary licenses and permits; Ensure compliance with applicable building regulations; Maintain safe working practices; Obtain required insurance; Correct defective workmanship at its own expense; and Deliver projects in accordance with agreed schedules.

Subcontractors

The Contractor may hire subcontractors at its own expense and remains fully responsible for workmanship, supervision, payment, safety, and compliance. The Employer has no employment relationship with subcontractors.

Payment Terms

Payments are by bank transfer under a fixed-fee contract. If work is incomplete, Customer may withhold payment.

Payment Schedule
Project Delays

Any work past the due date, including material and weather delays, is at the Contractor's cost.

Material Purchasing

Where practical, materials shall be purchased directly by the Estate or require prior written approval. Receipts are required for all reimbursable purchases.

Change Orders

No additional work shall proceed without written approval whenever project scope changes, project cost increases, or completion dates materially change. Verbal approvals are not valid.

Supervision

The Contractor must supervise all personnel and follow property rules, including a smoke- and drug-free workplace.

Right to Audit

The Employer reserves the right to review quotations, invoices, subcontractor agreements, labour records, material purchases, and project documentation for any work performed on behalf of the Estate.

Insurance

The Contractor shall maintain appropriate business insurance, including Commercial General Liability Insurance, Employer's Liability Insurance (where employees are engaged), Vehicle Insurance (if applicable), and any additional insurance reasonably required for the work.

Independent Business

The Contractor shall invoice separately, maintain separate accounting records, pay all applicable taxes, and maintain its own business registration. This Agreement does not entitle the Contractor to employment benefits.

Defective Work

The Contractor shall remedy defective workmanship discovered within the agreed warranty period at no additional cost, except where defects arise from owner-directed changes, normal wear, abuse, or events beyond the Contractor's control.

Additional Terms

The Contractor warrants all work for 6 months. Either party may cancel this agreement in writing at any time.

The Contractor will hold harmless and indemnify the Customer against all claims, except for willful misconduct or gross negligence. The Contractor must carry liability insurance for itself, subcontractors, and employees, and obtain all required licenses, permits, and inspections. Modifications to this contract require written agreement by both parties.
